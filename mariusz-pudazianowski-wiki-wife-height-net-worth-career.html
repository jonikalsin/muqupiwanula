Where was Mariusz Pudazianowski? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

On February 7, 1977, Mariusz Pudazianowski was born in Biala Rawska, Poland. He will be 45 years old in 2022. His parents were Wojciech Pudzianowski and Maria Pudzianowski.

Before changing careers to become a strength instructor, his father was a weightlifter. Sports and martial arts piqued Mariusz’s attention from a young age.

He started studying Kyokushin karate at the age of 11. He holds a 4th kyu green belt in Kyokushin karate. Mariusz started lifting weights when he was 13 years old.

At the age of 15, Mariusz started fighting; he stopped after seven years. He participated in the Polish weightlifting championship bench press competition at the age of 16 while participating in military sports training. For the next two years, he did not participate once more. On May 27, 2008, Mariusz earned his master’s degree in international relations.

How did Mariusz Pudazianowski start his Professional Career?

At the age of 21, he finally gave up martial arts to concentrate exclusively on the strongman competition. He also took home the title of Strongest Man in Poland, and in 2000, he competed in the World’s Strongest Man Contest for the first time, placing fourth behind Janne Virtanen, Magnus Samuelsson, and Svend Karlsen.

In 2002, Mariusz made a return. Although many believed he wouldn’t prevail, he unexpectedly took home the 2002 World’s Strongest Man crown.

He secured the title of World’s Strongest Man the following year, in 2003, with a 20-point advantage. He also won the Strongman Super Series World Championship in March 2004.

Although he placed third in the WSM, he was subsequently disqualified for violating the Organization’s Strongman Health policy. He had to give the reward money back as well. Additionally, for a year, Mariusz was not permitted to take part in the tournament.

The International Federation of Strength Athletes (IFSA) chose to split from WSM after ten years of cooperation. With a new sponsor on board, IFSA launched its tournament.

Three WSM titles were earned by Mariusz between 2005 and 2009. Because MMA needed different training, Mariusz said in an interview that the 2009 World’s Strongest Man Competition would be his last.

After dominating Strongman, Mariusz decided to take on a new task. MMA promoter KSW in Poland has hired Mariusz Pudziannowski. (Konfrontacja Sztuk Walk).

He made his début on December 11, 2009, in Warsaw, Poland, against Marcin Najman. By TKO, he took the victory in 43 seconds, earning him $70000. On May 7, 2010, Mariusz wrestled Yusuke Kamaguchi. He was chosen as the winner by all present.

He competed against Tim Sylvia, the former two-time UFC Heavyweight Champion, on May 21, 2010. By technical knockout in the second round (1:43), Slyvia prevailed.

After falling to Slyvia, Mariusz faced former heavyweight boxer and kickboxer Eric Esch, also known as Butterbean. He won the battle by submission in the opening round. He competed against James Thompson at KSW XVI and lost after James gave him an arm triangle.

The best MMA academy in America, American Top Team, trained Mariusz. He fought James Thompson again after his comeback, which he won by unanimous decision.

Thompson dominated Mariusz throughout the contest, making the win controversial. The KSW reversed the judgment and deemed the competition invalid. In KSW 31, which took place on May 23, 2015, in Gdansk, Poland, Mariusz faced Rolles Gracie Jr. First-round KO for Mariusz giving him the victory.

In the opening round, Mariusz hurt his knee and had to leave the competition. After leaving KSW for 15 months, he came back.

The opponent for Mariusz was the unbeaten Senegalese heavyweight Serigne Ousmane. On the day of the game, Ousmane experienced an acute appendicitis attack and was swiftly substituted by Serbia’s Nikola Milanovic.

Nikola was eliminated by him in the opening round. He competed against former KSW tourney champion Lukasz Jurkowski at KSW 63: Crime of the Century on June 5, 2021.

In the third round, he knocked Lukasz out to win. KSW has rescheduled the battle between Mariusz and Ousmane. At KSW 64, the battle took place on October 23, 2021. In the opening round, Mariusz eliminated Ousmane in just 18 seconds.

Strongman and MMA are hobbies for Mariusz, who claimed in an interview that he does not make much money from either of them. Along with running a truck cargo business called Pudzianowski Transport, Mariusz also runs a bodyguard academy.

He also invests in real estate. Other movies in which Mariusz Pudzianowski has acted include Neighborhooders, Nie ma takiego numeru, Faceci do wziecia and others.

He performed in the Pudzian Band, his sibling Krystian’s musical ensemble. Zdobyc Swiat, their debut single, and Dawaj na ring, their debut album, were both published in 2006. On the seventh Dancing with the Stars season, Mariusz made an appearance. Actress Magdalena Walach beat him in the championship round.
